SHED

Get rid of your cards. Don't lose the game.

House Rules

Objective

Be one of the first players to get rid of all your cards. The last player holding cards becomes the Shed — the loser.

Setup

  • Each player receives 3 face-down table cards (you may not look at these), 3 face-up table cards placed on top of them, and 3 hand cards.
  • With 2 players, you get 4 table cards each instead of 3.
  • Before play begins, you may swap any cards between your hand and your face-up table cards to set yourself up. When ready, the game starts.
  • The player holding the lowest non-special card (starting from 3) goes first. Ties broken by suit: clubs < spades < diamonds < hearts.

Taking Your Turn

  • Play one or more cards of the same rank onto the pile. The rank must be equal to or higher than the top card of the pile (unless a special rule applies).
  • If you cannot play, you must pick up the entire pile and add it to your hand.
  • After playing from your hand, draw back up to 3 cards while the draw pile lasts.

Phases of Play

You must exhaust each phase before moving to the next:

  1. Hand — play from the cards in your hand.
  2. Face-Up Table — once your hand is empty, play your visible table cards.
  3. Face-Down Table — flip one card at a time. If it plays, great. If not, pick up the pile and the flipped card.

Special Cards

CardEffect
2Wild — can be played on any card. Resets the pile; next player can play anything.
7Low cap — the next player must play a 7 or lower (or another special card).
8Skip — skips the next player. Playing multiple 8s skips multiple players.
10Burn — clears the entire pile immediately. The same player plays again.
JokerReversal — reverses the direction of play. Always playable on any card. (6-player games only.)

Four of a Kind Burn

If the top four cards of the pile share the same rank — whether played at once or built up over multiple turns — the pile burns automatically. The player who triggered it plays again.

Mixed Play

Once the draw pile is gone, if you have matching cards in both your hand and your face-up table cards, you may play them all together in a single move.
